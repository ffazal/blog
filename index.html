Banff National Park

Banff National Park was founded in 1885 and is Canada's oldest national park. Banff is a hilly region in Alberta's Rocky Mountains, located 110–180 kilometres west of Calgary. It has several glaciers and ice fields, dense coniferous forest, and alpine panoramas. Banff has a long history as one of the most awe-inspiring alpine locations in the world.

Jasper National Park

In Alberta, Canada, Jasper National Park is a national park. With an area of 11,000 km2, it is the biggest national park in Alberta's Rocky Mountains (4,200 sq mi). It is situated to the north of Banff National Park and to the west of Edmonton. The Columbia Icefield glaciers, as well as springs, lakes, waterfalls, and mountains, are all included inside the park.

Yoho National Park

Yoho National Park is located in eastern British Columbia, Canada, amid the Rocky Mountains. Wapta Falls, on the Kicking Horse River, and the colossal Takakkaw Falls are among the many waterfalls. Emerald Lake is surrounded by a walkway that around it, with a natural stone bridge close. Alpine lakes, cliffs, and woodland pathways may be found in the Lake O'Hara region.

Waterton Lakes National Park

Waterton Lakes National Park is a national park in Alberta, Canada. It is located in the southwest portion of the province. It shares a boundary with Montana's Glacier National Park. Waterton National Park was established in 1895 and was named after Waterton Lake, which was named after Victorian naturalist and environmentalist Charles Waterton. Its range extends from the Rocky Mountains to the plains.

Kootenay National Park

Kootenay National Park, located in southern British Columbia's Rocky Mountains, is a place of breathtaking landscape and plentiful wildlife. Towering mountains and hanging glaciers meet tiny chasms, vast wooded valleys, parched grasslands, and color-splashed mineral lakes throughout the park's long narrow contour.

Bruce Peninsula National Park

The beautiful park, which spans 155 square kilometres near the point of the Niagara Escarpment in southern Ontario, is made up of limestone cliffs, caverns and subterranean streams, and ancient woods with some of Canada's oldest trees. The Bruce Peninsula National Park contains a diverse range of ecosystems, including uncommon Alvars, deep woods, and pristine lakes.

Gros Morne National Park

Gros Morne National Park is located in eastern Canada on the west coast of the island of Newfoundland. A route leads from Gros Morne, a summit in the Long Range Mountains, to the Ten Mile Pond valley. Western Brook Pond is a glacier-carved fjord featuring cliffs and waterfalls, notably Pissing Mare Falls.

Kluane National Park

Kluane National Park and Reserve is located in Canada's Yukon Territory. It's a wide landscape of ice fields, woods, and towering peaks such as Mount Logan. The King's Throne trail, which goes over Kathleen Lake to a beautiful cirque, is one of the trails available. The Alsek River, which is fed by glaciers, runs through grizzly bear territory.

Gaspesie National Park

Gaspésie National Park is a provincial park in the inland of the Gaspé peninsula, located south of Sainte-Anne-des-Monts, Quebec, Canada. Mont Jacques-Cartier, Canada's highest mountain in the Appalachian Mountains at 1,270 metres above sea level, is located inside the park. Gros Morne's historic scenery is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, shaped by colliding continents and grinding glaciers.

Pacific Rim National Park

Long Beach, the Broken Group Islands, and the West Coast Trail are all part of Pacific Rim National Park Reserve, a 511-square-kilometer park in British Columbia, Canada. Rugged beaches and temperate rainforests define the Pacific Coast Mountains. The Pacific Rim is known for its ancient rainforests, abundant marine life, and violent winter storms.
